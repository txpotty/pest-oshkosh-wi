Common Signs of a Mosquito Infestation

One of the most apparent signs is, of course, the sheer number of mosquitoes present on your property. If you step outside, particularly during dawn or dusk, and are immediately surrounded by buzzing and biting insects, you likely have a problem. Another indicator is finding mosquito larvae in standing water around your home. These 'wrigglers' are easily visible and confirmation that mosquitoes are actively breeding nearby. Additionally, if you are experiencing an unusual number of mosquito bites, even after brief periods outdoors, it's a strong signal that professional intervention may be necessary. Mosquito activity might also be noticeable around shaded, humid areas of your yard, or near areas with dense vegetation.

Health Risks Associated with Mosquitoes

Beyond the irritating bites and itchy welts, mosquitoes are vectors for several diseases. While not all mosquitoes carry diseases, the potential for transmission is a serious concern. In Wisconsin, while not as common as in other parts of the world, mosquitoes can transmit West Nile Virus (WNV). Symptoms of WNV can range from mild, flu-like symptoms to severe neurological illness. By reducing the mosquito population on your property, you are also reducing the potential risk of exposure to these diseases for your family and pets. Other mosquito-borne illnesses found in the United States, though less prevalent in our immediate area, include Eastern Equine Encephalitis (EEE) and La Crosse Encephalitis (LACV). Protecting your home and loved ones through effective mosquito control is an important step in safeguarding public health within the Oshkosh community and beyond, encompassing areas like Neenah.

The Mosquito Lifecycle Explained

Understanding the mosquito lifecycle is key to effective control. Mosquitoes undergo complete metamorphosis, progressing through four distinct stages: egg, larva, pupa, and adult. Eggs are typically laid on the surface of water or in areas that will flood. When water is present, the eggs hatch into larvae, often called 'wrigglers.' These aquatic larvae feed on organic matter in the water and breathe air through a siphon at the water's surface. After maturing, larvae develop into pupae, the 'tumblers.' The pupal stage is a non-feeding stage where the mosquito undergoes transformation into an adult. Finally, the adult mosquito emerges from the pupal skin and flies off. The entire lifecycle can take anywhere from a few days to several weeks, depending on temperature and species. Our treatments target both the larval and adult stages to break the cycle effectively in the Oshkosh area.

The Science Behind Mosquito Control Products

The products we use for mosquito control are carefully selected for their effectiveness and safety when applied according to label instructions. Larvicides target mosquito larvae in water and prevent them from reaching the adult stage. These can be biological products, like those containing the bacteria Bacillus thuringiensis israelensis (Bti), which is toxic specifically to mosquito and black fly larvae, or insect growth regulators that disrupt their development. Adulticides are used to reduce the population of biting adult mosquitoes. These may include pyrethroids, synthetic insecticides based on natural compounds found in chrysanthemum flowers, or other effective formulations. We are knowledgeable about the different types of products available and choose the most appropriate ones for your situation in Oshkosh, always prioritizing safety and effectiveness. This scientific understanding is vital for addressing challenging pest issues such as tick control.
